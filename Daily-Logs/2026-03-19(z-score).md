## 1. Core Concepts: Inferential Statistics

### Z-Scores & Standardization
* **Standard Normal Distribution:** A distribution where $mean = 0$ and $std.dev = 1$.
* **Z-Score Formula:** $$Z = \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma}$$
* **Purpose:** Used to compare two different normal distributions even if they have different means and deviations.

### Coefficient of Variation (CV)
* **Definition:** A measure used to determine the relative spread of data.
* **Formula:** $$CV = \frac{\sigma}{\mu}$$

### The Central Limit Theorem (CLT)
* The CLT states that as the sample size becomes larger, the distribution of the sample means will approach a normal distribution, regardless of the population's distribution shape.

### Confidence Intervals & Margin of Error
* **Confidence Interval:** A range calculation showing how confidently we believe a population mean falls within a range based on a sample mean.
* **Margin of Error (E):** $$E = \pm Z \cdot \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}$$
  * $s$ = Standard Deviation
  * $n$ = Number of samples
* **Formula:** $$Confidence \ Interval = Sample \ Mean \pm Margin \ of \ Error (E)$$

### Hypothesis Testing & P-Values
* **P-Value:** The probability of something occurring by chance rather than because of the hypothesized explanation. 
  * $P < 0.05$ is generally considered **Statistically Significant**.
* **Hypothesis Types:**
    * **Null Hypothesis ($H_0$):** The default assumption (no effect).
    * **Alternate Hypothesis ($H_1$ or $H_2$):** What you are trying to prove.
* **Test Types:**
    * **One-Tailed Test:** $H_0: \mu \ge mean$, $H_1: \mu < mean$.
    * **Two-Tailed Test:** $H_0: \mu = 10$, $H_1: \mu \neq 10$.

### T-Distribution
* Used when dealing with **small samples**.
* **Characteristics:** Similar to a normal distribution but with **flatter tails**, indicating more variance and uncertainty.
* Uses a **Critical t-value** instead of a Critical z-value.

## 2. Python Code Implementation


```python
import numpy as np
from scipy import stats

# --- 1. Calculating Z-Score ---
data = [10, 12, 14, 16, 18, 20]
mean = np.mean(data)
std_dev = np.std(data)

# Z-score for a specific value (e.g., 18)
z_score = (18 - mean) / std_dev
print(f"Z-score of 18: {z_score:.2f}")

# --- 2. Confidence Interval Calculation ---
confidence = 0.95
sample_mean = 50
sample_std = 5
n = 30

# Calculate Margin of Error using Z-distribution
z_critical = stats.norm.ppf((1 + confidence) / 2)
margin_of_error = z_critical * (sample_std / np.sqrt(n))

confidence_interval = (sample_mean - margin_of_error, sample_mean + margin_of_error)
print(f"95% Confidence Interval: {confidence_interval}")

# --- 3. Hypothesis Testing (P-Value) ---
# T-test for a small sample
sample_data = [14, 15, 12, 16, 14, 13, 15]
null_mean = 15

t_stat, p_value = stats.ttest_1samp(sample_data, null_mean)
print(f"P-value: {p_value:.4f}")

if p_value < 0.05:
    print("Statistically Significant: Reject the Null Hypothesis")
else:
    print("Not Statistically Significant: Fail to Reject the Null Hypothesis")
