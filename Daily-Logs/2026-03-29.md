## 1. Theoretical Concepts: Probability Distributions

### Binomial Distribution
Used when there are only two possible outcomes (Success/Failure).
* **Conditions:** $p + q = 1$ and $p, q$ are constant across trials.
* **PMF (Probability Mass Function):** $P(X = x) = ^nC_x \cdot p^x \cdot q^{n-x}$
* **CDF (Cumulative Distribution Function):** $P(X \le x) = \sum_{k=0}^{x} ^nC_k \cdot p^k \cdot (1-p)^{n-k}$
* **Mean:** $n \cdot p$
* **Variance:** $n \cdot p \cdot (1-p)$


### Poisson Distribution
Models the number of events occurring over a fixed period of time or space.
* **PMF:** $P(X = k) = \frac{e^{-\lambda} \cdot \lambda^k}{k!}$
* **$\lambda$:** Rate of occurrence per unit measurement.
* **Mean & Variance:** Both are equal to $\lambda$.

### Exponential Distribution
A continuous distribution used for modeling the time between independent events (e.g., time-to-failure of electronic components).
* **PDF (Probability Density Function):** $f(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x}, \quad x \ge 0$
* **Mean:** $1/\lambda$

### Normal Distribution (Gaussian)
The "Bell Curve" characterized by symmetry around the mean.
* **Standardization (Z-score):** $Z = \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma}$
* **Confidence Interval:** A range used to estimate a population parameter with a specific level of confidence (alpha).
